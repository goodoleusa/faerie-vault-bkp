# Sales Pitch Script

This is a structured script for a 15-minute first meeting with a pharmacy franchise decision-maker — a regional director, franchise owner, or VP of Operations. It covers the problem, solution, proof, pricing, and close. Adapt the language to your voice — this is a framework, not a teleprompter.

**Format:** In-person or video call. Have the demo site open in a browser tab before you start.

---

## Before the Call — Setup

1. Confirm their location count and approximate daily prescription volume (ask before the call or check their website)
2. Have the demo site ready: `[your demo URL]` — tested, loading fast
3. Know their rough pricing tier going in (1 location vs. 3–5 vs. 6+)
4. Have [[00-SHARED/Human-Inbox/hustle/08-DEMO-SITE-CHECKLIST]] completed — the demo should already be live

---

## Opening — 2 Minutes

**Goal:** Establish relevance fast. Don't start with features.

---

"Thanks for making time. I want to use these 15 minutes to show you something specific — not a general pitch. Let me ask you one thing first:

How many calls does your pharmacy staff handle in a day that are just people asking if their prescription is ready?"

*(Let them answer. They will say a number. It will be higher than they expected when they say it out loud.)*

"Right. And each one of those calls takes 2–3 minutes of a technician's time. Multiply that by every location, every day — and you're paying for a call center that you don't need to be running.

That's what I want to show you how to fix."

---

## Problem — 2 Minutes

**Goal:** Make them feel the pain, not just describe it.

---

"Here's the pattern I see across pharmacy networks: the website is an afterthought. It has your hours, your address, maybe a contact form. It's basically a digital business card.

Meanwhile, your staff is fielding calls about wait times, prescription status, delivery requests — all things that could be self-served online. The information exists in your system. Patients just can't see it.

So they call. You answer. You explain. You hang up. You answer again.

Your technicians went to school for clinical work. They're doing call center work instead."

*(Pause. Let that land.)*

"The fix isn't complicated. It's a website that actually works as a customer service tool."

---

## Solution — 3 Minutes

**Goal:** Show, don't tell. Pull up the demo.

---

"Let me show you what I mean. This is a demo of RxReady — what a patient at your pharmacy would see."

*(Pull up the demo site.)*

"First: live queue status. Each zone in your waiting area gets a display: 'Zone 1: ~12 minutes.' Patients can see this before they come in. They can decide to drop off and come back, or they can wait.

No one calls to ask how long the wait is if they can see it in 5 seconds.

Second: prescription lookup. A patient enters their Rx number and date of birth. They see 'Ready for pickup' or 'Processing' or 'Insurance pending.' That's it. No PHI, no drug names — just the status they need.

Third: delivery booking. A simple form. They submit it, your staff gets an email notification, they fulfill it in your existing system. No new workflow for your team.

And now let me show you what you see as the operator."

*(Switch to admin dashboard.)*

"This is your heatmap. Every location, every hour of the day, for the past 7 days. When are you busiest? Which location has the longest waits on Tuesday afternoons? You've never had this data before. Now you do."

---

## Proof — 2 Minutes

**Goal:** One specific, credible example.

---

"Mesa Verde Pharmacy Network — 3 locations in the Southwest, about 2,500 daily prescriptions across the network.

Before we built this: 15% of their customers checked anything online. Staff estimated 40–60 status calls per location per day.

90 days after launch: 60% of customers were using the site for status lookups. Inbound calls dropped to 20–30 per day per location. They recovered about 2 hours of technician time per day across the network.

Their words, not mine: 'We saved two full-time positions in call handling without laying anyone off — we just redeployed them to the pharmacy floor.'

That's the typical outcome. Not a best case."

---

## Pricing — 1 Minute

**Goal:** Make it feel like an obvious decision, not a negotiation.

---

"For a network your size — [X locations] — we're looking at a [setup fee range] upfront and [retainer range] per month.

Let me put that in context: at 15% call reduction, you recover that retainer cost in labor savings within 60 days. After that, it's profit — plus you have a modern digital presence your competitors don't have.

The setup fee pays for everything: site design, queue system setup, Cloudflare configuration, your admin dashboard, and your staff training video. One payment, and you're live in 4 weeks."

---

## Objection Handling

These are the objections you will hear. Have answers ready without hesitating.

---

**"Isn't this HIPAA-regulated? We can't put patient data on a website."**

"That's exactly the right question — and the reason RxReady is designed the way it is. We never store patient data. Your pharmacy system stays the source of truth. The website just displays status strings: 'Ready,' 'Processing,' 'Insurance pending.' No names, no drug details, no insurance information.

Because we don't touch PHI, we're not a HIPAA Business Associate — and you don't need a BAA with us. We provide that in writing at contract signing. Your compliance officer gets a full data flow diagram and the Worker source code to review before anything goes live."

---

**"What if your service goes down? We can't have patients unable to access their pharmacy."**

"Great concern. Two answers:

First, we host on Cloudflare Pages — they have 99.99% historical uptime and a global network. If anything goes wrong, it's at a level of infrastructure reliability that your hospital systems don't have.

Second, the important thing: if the website goes down, the pharmacy doesn't go down. The queue display just disappears — patients don't see wait times. But they can still call, still walk in, still get their prescriptions. The website is a convenience layer, not a critical path. Your operations are completely independent of it."

---

**"How long does setup take? We've been burned by tech projects before."**

"4 weeks, start to finish. And I'll tell you exactly what those 4 weeks look like.

Week 1: you send me your logo, your photos, your copy, and your API endpoint details. We have a checklist — it's about 2 hours of your team's time.

Weeks 2–3: I build and configure everything. You get a staging URL to review.

Week 4: your compliance officer signs off, we do a staff-only soft launch, then go live.

The 4-week timeline assumes your team gets me the Week 1 materials on time. I've never missed the 4-week mark when that happens."

---

**"We already have a website. Why would we replace it?"**

"You don't have to replace it — we can build on top of what you have, or build the RxReady components as additions. But honestly? Most pharmacy websites I see are 3–5 years old, not mobile-optimized, and built by a designer who knew nothing about pharmacy operations.

Part of what I'm offering is a fresh start: a site that loads in under 2 seconds on mobile, looks professional, and actually serves your patients as a tool — not just a listing.

If you want to keep your existing site and just add the queue features, I'm happy to scope that instead."

---

**"We're not sure about the budget right now."**

"Understood. A few things worth knowing:

One: the ROI is documented. I can walk you through the math for your specific location count and prescription volume — it's a 60-90 day payback on the retainer.

Two: I offer 12-month and 24-month contracts. The 24-month term gets a 10% discount on the setup fee, which can help if upfront budget is tight.

Three: if you want to start with a single pilot location, we can do that. Test the model, see the call volume data, then expand. Single-location pricing applies until you're ready to bundle."

---

## Close

**Goal:** Get to a next step, not a yes/no decision.

---

"Here's what I'd suggest: let me spin up a custom demo using your brand colors and your location names — takes me about 48 hours. You share it with your team, your compliance officer, whoever needs to weigh in.

If it looks right and the numbers work, we sign a contract and start Week 1. If it doesn't, you've spent nothing except 15 minutes today.

When would you want to see the custom demo?"

*(Stop talking. Wait for their answer.)*
