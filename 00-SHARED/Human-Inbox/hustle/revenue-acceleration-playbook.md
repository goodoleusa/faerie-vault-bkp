## First 5 Sales + Recurring Revenue Strategy

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

You have a complete product, correct pricing, and zero clients. The gap is not product — it is sales motion. This playbook closes that gap with specific actions, ordered by speed to cash.

**This week's single target: 1 signed client, Stripe link clicked, $799 in the bank.**

## 1. FIRST 5 SALES — THIS WEEK

### 48-Hour Pre-Sales Asset Sprint

Before any outreach, build the credibility kit:

**Day 1 (4 hours total):**
- Build "Ridge Peak Contracting" on the FoundationCraft template. Live URL (~$12 domain). Deploy to Cloudflare Pages. Run PageSpeed. Screenshot the 95+ score. This is your entire portfolio until you have a real client.
- Set up Stripe recurring products: $79/mo "Starter Hosting + Maintenance", $129/mo "Growth", $199/mo "Full-Service." One-time 15 minutes. This wires your MRR from the first sale.

**Day 2 (3 hours total):**
- Audit PDF template in Canva or Google Slides. Fields: business name, current PageSpeed vs. what you'd deliver, mobile grade, GBP completeness, 1 recommendation. Fillable in 15 minutes per prospect.
- Build your warm prospect list: 10-20 local businesses you personally patronize, know, or can get introduced to within 48 hours.

**Day 3 (1 hour):**
- One-page service agreement in Google Doc. What's included, what's not, payment terms, 90-day cancellation. Not a lawyer-drafted contract. Keeps deals moving.

### The 5 Paths to Signed Client

**Path 1 — Warm Handshake (zero cost, closes fastest)**

Text or call every warm contact: "Hey [name], I'm launching a web design service for local businesses and doing free audits this week. Can I spend 10 minutes showing you what a $799 redesign would look like for [business]?" Ten yeses yields 2-3 paid clients. No other channel closes faster because trust is already established.

**Path 2 — Postcard Sniper (~$50)**

Pull the county contractor license database (most states publish free online at the licensing board website). Export 50-100 business names and addresses within 20 miles. Postcard message: "[Business name] — your website is costing you jobs. I'll prove it free. Text AUDIT to [your number] or scan this QR code." QR links to a Google Form. VistaPrint 50 postcards: ~$40-50.

**Path 3 — Facebook Local Group Drop (zero cost)**

Join every "Support Local [City]", "Small Biz [City]", "[City] Entrepreneurs" Facebook group. Post once per group: "Doing 5 free website audits for local businesses this week — no pitch, just a real report. Anyone interested?" High DM conversion because you opened with value, not a sales pitch.

**Path 4 — GBP Cold Call (zero cost)**

Google "contractor [your city]." Find businesses with no website or a visibly outdated site. Call directly: "Hi, I'm [name], I do web design for local contractors in [city]. I noticed your site [scores a 41 on Google's speed test / doesn't exist]. I did a quick audit and have one specific thing costing you leads. Can I send the report?" Follow up 48 hours later.

**Path 5 — BNI/Chamber Seat (Week 1, low cost)**

Call every BNI chapter in your metro. Ask if the web design seat is open. One chapter with 30 members means 30 people whose job it is to send you referrals. Chamber membership ($200-500/year) unlocks the same pool. Doesn't close this week, but builds the pipeline that sustains months 3-6.

### Close Mechanics — When They Say "I'm Interested"

1. Schedule a 20-minute screen share immediately.
2. Show: demo site, PageSpeed score, their current site vs. yours side by side.
3. Say: "I can have your site live in 5 days. Setup is $799. I'll send you the Stripe link right now — once that clears I start tomorrow."
4. Send the Stripe link before hanging up. Do not leave the call without sending it.

If they hesitate on $799 upfront: offer $400 now + $400 in 30 days. Same total. No discount. Only when needed to close — don't advertise it.

## 2. RECURRING REVENUE — WIRE IT NOW

**The gap:** Monthly billing is not automatic. You must wire it at the first sale.

**Step 1 (already covered above):** Create Stripe recurring products for each tier.

**Step 2:** When the client pays the $799 setup fee, include the subscription link in the same email:
> "Your site goes live within 5 days of your setup payment clearing. The $79/month hosting plan starts on your launch date — click here to set it up now: [Stripe link]"

**Step 3:** They subscribe once. Stripe auto-charges monthly. You receive an email if a card fails. Zero manual work per billing cycle.

**The pitch (say this before they ask):**
> "The $799 gets your site built and live. The $79/month covers hosting, SSL, DNS, uptime monitoring, and unlimited minor updates — hours changes, photo swaps, copy tweaks. Anything under 30 minutes is included. Cancel with 90 days notice."

**The $49/month save:** If a client balks at $79/month, offer "watch-only" monitoring at $49/month — you alert them if anything breaks, they handle updates. Don't lead with this. It is a save, not an offer.

## 4. VERTICAL FOCUS — ORDERED BY SALES CYCLE

### Beachhead: Contractors (FoundationCraft) — Close in 2-3 Days

The owner IS the decision-maker. No committee, no approval chain. ROI math is immediate: one extra job per month pays for the site five times over. The before/after gallery demo closes the visual case on the call. County license database is a ready-made prospect list. Highest SMB density in any metro.

**Do this first. Do not split focus.**

### Second: Restaurants (TableTurn) — Close in 3-5 Days

Reachable 2-4pm during off-peak. The inline menu vs. PDF pitch is visceral: "Google can't index your menu because it's a PDF — every item you serve is invisible to search engines." High density, constant new-opening pipeline. Slightly more setup friction (menu formatting).

### Third: Solo Attorneys (CounselClear) — Close in 5-10 Days

Slower buyers but they pay without blinking. $1,499-$2,299 setup + $199/month. ADA compliance is the opener — attorneys understand liability exposure. Moderate density but exceptional unit economics.

### Skip for First 5 Sales:
- **Pharmacy (RxReady):** HIPAA conversations, longer sales cycle, compliance officer involvement. Pursue after contractor cash flow is stable and you have 3+ clients. The unit economics are exceptional ($249-399/month retainer) — it's worth the wait.
- **Delivery, Association, Logistics:** Lower density, higher friction, pursue opportunistically.

## 5. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

### What's Right

**$799 Starter — correct. Do not change.** Sits in the "I can say yes today without calling my accountant" zone for any business doing $100K+/year. Below $499 signals low quality. Above $999 triggers the three-quote reflex. $799 is the decision-threshold sweet spot.

**$1,299-$2,499 — correct, but requires demo-based selling.** No one pays $2,499 on a description. The demo IS the close. Without showing it, these tiers face objection.

### Loss Leader: Audit, Not Site

Do NOT discount the site. Discounting trains clients to expect discounts and signals the price was inflated.

Instead, use these as loss leaders:
- **Free website audit PDF** (15 minutes of work, creates obligation, demonstrates expertise)
- **$99 GBP Optimization** sold standalone — low commitment, immediate value, opens the retainer conversation: "Now that your GBP is sorted, want me to build the site that matches it?"

### Anchor Language

- vs. Wix: "A Wix Business plan is $35/month — generic template, no support. We're $79/month, your site outperforms anything on Wix, and I answer texts."
- vs. agency: "A local agency quoted you $8,000? We deliver the same quality in a week for $799."
- vs. Fiverr: "That Fiverr site? We can rebuild it properly for $799 and it'll actually rank on Google."

## 6. AUTOMATION VS. PERSONAL TOUCH

### Automate Immediately

| Task | Tool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly billing | Stripe recurring subscription | Converts one-time payment to MRR, zero manual work |
| Client intake | Tally.so or Google Form | Consistent data collection |
| Uptime alerts | UptimeRobot (free) | Reactive, not proactive — you find out before the client |
| Invoicing | Wave (free) or Stripe | No manual invoice creation |
| Form submissions | Formspree → email | Already built into templates |

### Keep Personal — This IS the Moat

| Task | Why personal |
|---|---|
| Initial audit delivery | Deliver by call — the call IS the sales conversation |
| Onboarding call | Sets expectations, prevents scope creep, builds relationship |
| Launch day message | "Your site is live" + a personal note creates loyalty |
| Monthly 5-minute check-in | Prevents churn better than any feature or contract clause |
| Referral ask | Personal ask converts 3x better than automated email |

The differentiator from Wix, Squarespace, and any SaaS competitor is the human layer. Automate the accounting. Keep the relationship.

## 7. PORTFOLIO AND SOCIAL PROOF — ZERO CLIENTS

### The Demo Site Strategy

Build fake-business demo sites that look real. Host them live. Use them in every sales conversation.

**Minimum viable portfolio (build this week):**
1. "Ridge Peak Contracting" — FoundationCraft template, live URL, real PageSpeed screenshot
2. "Mesa Verde Kitchen" — TableTurn template, live URL, inline menu, OpenTable embed

No one checks whether "Ridge Peak Contracting" is real. They see a professional site scoring 97 on PageSpeed and they want one.

### Five Credibility Accelerators (Zero Clients Required)

1. **Before/after screenshots:** Find a local contractor with a genuinely bad website (dozens exist in every city). Screenshot it. Build a FoundationCraft version. Show both. "Here's where you are. Here's where you'd be in 5 days."

2. **PageSpeed comparison:** Screenshot your demo (97) vs. a local business's Wix/Squarespace site (64). Post to your GBP, LinkedIn, Facebook group. Objective data requires no client permission.

3. **Hypothetical case study:** "How a local HVAC contractor could get 3 more leads per month with a better website." Write it. Post it. Positions you as vertical expert before you have a single client.

4. **First client testimonial:** After launch #1 — over-deliver, then ask for a one-line Google review within 48 hours of go-live. One real review unlocks 5 more conversations.

5. **Screenshot everything:** Every PageSpeed score, every successful form test, every launch. These are your social proof collateral for the next 6 months.
